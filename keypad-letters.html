Telephone Keypad Reference

The letters on your
phone keypad.

Every number key from 2 to 9 carries three or four letters — the system behind vanity numbers, T9 texting, and "press 2 for ABC". Here's the full map and the story of how it got there.

The Letter Map

What letters go with each number?

On every modern telephone keypad, the digits 2 through 9 each carry a group of letters: 2 = ABC, 3 = DEF, 4 = GHI, 5 = JKL, 6 = MNO, 7 = PQRS, 8 = TUV, 9 = WXYZ. The digits 1 and 0 carry no letters at all.

Where It Came From

Why phones have letters at all

The letters predate the keypad itself — they were how people dialed before all-number calling.

1920s — exchange names

Numbers were dialed as words: "PEnnsylvania 6-5000" meant dialing P-E, then 6-5000. Letters on the rotary dial made whole telephone exchanges memorable.

1963 — the touch-tone grid

Bell's push-button phones kept the letters in the now-familiar 3×4 grid. The ITU later standardized the map worldwide — adding Q to 7 and Z to 9, which old dials had skipped.

1990s — T9 and texting

The same letter groups powered T9 predictive text — tap 4-3-5-5-6 and the phone guessed "hello." Today the map lives on in vanity numbers and phone menus.

The Missing Keys

Why 0 and 1 have no letters

On early rotary systems, 1 was reserved for signaling and long-distance prefixes and 0 rang the operator — so neither digit was ever assigned letters. That's why every phoneword is built only from digits 2 through 9, and why 0s and 1s pass through a vanity number unchanged (the 1-800 in 1-800-FLOWERS is always dialed as digits).

FAQ

Keypad letter questions, answered

What letters correspond to each number on a phone?
2 = ABC, 3 = DEF, 4 = GHI, 5 = JKL, 6 = MNO, 7 = PQRS, 8 = TUV, and 9 = WXYZ. The digits 1 and 0 have no letters.
Why do phone keypads have letters?
Letters were added in the 1920s so callers could dial memorable exchange names like "PEnnsylvania 6-5000" instead of raw digits. The system survived into touch-tone phones and now powers vanity numbers, T9-style texting, and "press 2 for ABC" phone menus.
Where are Q and Z on the phone keypad?
Q is on the 7 key and Z is on the 9 key. Old rotary dials skipped both letters; the modern ITU E.161 standard added them so every letter of the alphabet can be dialed.
Which numbers have no letters?
1 and 0. On early phone systems 1 was reserved for signaling and long-distance prefixes and 0 rang the operator, so neither was ever assigned letters — they always stay as digits in a phoneword.
How do I type letters as a phone number?
