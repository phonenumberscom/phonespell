    The Short Answer

    What does your phone number spell?

    Every digit from 2 through 9 on a telephone keypad maps to three or four letters, so almost any phone number can spell words — known as phonewords. Type your number into the decoder above and it checks every possible letter combination against a large dictionary, instantly listing each word and phrase your digits spell.

    Full spellings vs. partial matches

    A full spelling converts every changeable digit into one word or phrase — the way 1-800-356-9377 becomes 1-800-FLOWERS. Partial matches turn just a few digits into a word, like 555-CARE, which are often the most practical phone number spellings for a local line. The decoder ranks full spellings first, then the strongest partial words.

    What if my number spells nothing?

    Numbers heavy in 0s and 1s may spell nothing at all, since those digits carry no letters. If that's you, flip the tool to Word → Number: pick the word you wish your number spelled, convert it to digits, and search our marketplace for a real phone number that matches — something a spelling tool alone can't do.

    FAQ

    Vanity number questions, answered

    Everything about decoding, spelling, and buying memorable phone numbers.

    What is a vanity phone number?
    A vanity number — also called a phoneword — spells a word or phrase on the phone keypad, like 1-800-FLOWERS, which makes it far easier to remember and share than a string of random digits.
    What does my phone number spell?
    Type your number into the decoder above. We map each digit to its keypad letters (2=ABC, 3=DEF, and so on) and surface every real word or phrase your number can spell.
    How does the phone number decoder work?
    Digits 2 through 9 each map to three or four letters. The tool checks those letter combinations against a large dictionary to find memorable words, then ranks the best vanity spellings.
    Can I turn a word into a phone number?

    What do the letters on a phone keypad stand for?
    Each digit from 2 to 9 carries a group of letters: 2 = ABC, 3 = DEF, 4 = GHI, 5 = JKL, 6 = MNO, 7 = PQRS, 8 = TUV, 9 = WXYZ. The layout dates to early Bell telephone exchanges and is the same mapping T9 texting used — it's what makes phonewords possible.
    Why do 0 and 1 have no letters?
    On early rotary phones, 1 was reserved for signaling and long-distance prefixes and 0 for the operator, so neither was ever assigned letters. In any phone number spelling, 0 and 1 stay as digits.
